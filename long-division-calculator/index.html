What is Long Division?

Long division is a fundamental arithmetic algorithm used to divide large numbers (the dividend) by smaller numbers (the divisor). Unlike mental division, which breaks a number into equal parts instantly, long division breaks the problem down into a sequence of simple, repetitive steps.

This method is essential for students in 3rd, 4th, and 5th grade as it teaches place value, logical sequencing, and arithmetic patience. Our calculator serves as a private tutor, visualizing each step so you can check your homework or learn the method from scratch.

The Three Methods Explained

1. US/UK Method (Bracket)

This is the standard notation used in North America and the UK. The divisor is placed to the left of a bracket `)`, and the dividend is placed inside (under the "roof"). The quotient (answer) is written on top.

2. European Method (Galley)

Common in France, Spain, Italy, and Latin America. The dividend and divisor are separated by a vertical line `|`. The quotient is written in the L-shaped nook on the right.

3. German Method (Colon)

Common in Germany and Northern Europe. The problem is written as a linear equation (e.g., 1250 : 5 = 250). The subtraction work happens directly underneath the dividend.

How to Perform Long Division (Step-by-Step)

The easiest way to remember the long division process is the mnemonic "Dad, Mom, Sister, Brother" (DMSB):

  1. D (Divide): Look at the first digit of the dividend. How many times does the divisor fit into it? Write that number on top.
  2. M (Multiply): Multiply the number you just wrote on top by the divisor. Write the result underneath the dividend digit.
  3. S (Subtract): Subtract that result from the dividend digit to find the difference.
  4. B (Bring Down): Bring down the next digit of the dividend to the right of your subtraction result. Now, repeat the process with this new number!

Let's apply the steps:

  • Divide: 5 does not go into 1. So, look at 17. 5 goes into 17 3 times. Write "3" on top.
  • Multiply: 3 ร 5 = 15. Write 15 under the 17.
  • Subtract: 17 - 15 = 2.
  • Bring Down: Bring down the 5 next to the 2. The new number is 25.
  • Repeat: 5 goes into 25 exactly 5 times. Write "5" on top. The final answer is 35.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a remainder?

A remainder is the amount "left over" after division. For example, if you have 7 apples and share them between 2 people, each gets 3 apples, and 1 apple is left over. The answer is 3 R 1.

Can I divide by zero?

No. Division by zero is undefined in mathematics. If you try entering "0" in the divisor field, the calculator will alert you that this operation is impossible.
